✨ The process of material deposition onto a heated substrate through decomposition or chemical reaction of compounds contained gas passing over the substrate.

CVD Film Growth Steps

  • Reactant gasses enter the reactor and gasses diffuse through the boundary layer.
  • Gasses come in contact with surface of substrate and adsorption takes place.
  • Chemical reaction / decomposition reaction takes place on surface of substrate, deposition.
  • Gasesous by-products of the reaction are diffused out of surface by carrier gas.

CVD Geometries

  • Horizontal Reactor
  • Vertical Reactor
  • Cylindrical Reactor
Example: Preparation of \(TiB_2\), melting point is \(3325^\circ \text{ C}\), deposited by CVD at \(1000^\circ \text{ C}\).
✦
\(TiCl_4\) \(+\) \(2BCl_3\) \(+\) \(5H_2\) \(\rightarrow\) \(TiB_2\) \(+\) \(10 HCl\)

Precursors

  • Liquid rather than solid or gaseous.
  • Good volatility.
  • Good thermal stability in the delivery system, during evaporation and transport.
  • Decompose cleanly and controllably on the substrate without incorporation.
  • Give stable by-products which are readily removed from the reaction zone.
  • Readily available in consistent quality and quantity at low cost.
  • Non-toxic and non-pyrophoric.

Types of CVD Reactions

  • Thermal decomposition
  • Reduction
  • Oxidation and nitridation
  • Exchange
  • Disproportionation

Thermal Decomposition Reaction

✦
\(AB_{(g)}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(A_{(s)}\) \(+\) \(B_{(g)}\)
Example: Si deposition from Silane at \(650^\circ \text{ C}\).
✦
\({SiH_4}_{(g)}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(Si_{(s)}\) \(+\) \(2{H_2}_{(g)}\)

Reduction Reaction

Often using \(H_2\) (metal, substrate), lower temperature than pyrolysis reversible, can use for cleaning too.
✦
\(AX_{(g)}\) \(+\) \({H_2}_{(g)}\) \(\rightleftharpoons\) \(A_{(s)}\) \(+\) \(HX_{(g)}\)
Example:
✦
\({WF_6}_{(g)}\) \(+\) \(3{H_2}_{(g)}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(W_{(s)}\) \(+\) \(6HF_{(g)}\) \(300^\circ \text{ C}\)
✦
\({MoF_6}_{(g)}\) \(+\) \({H_2}_{(g)}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(Mo_{(s)}\) \(+\) \(6HF_{(g)}\) \(300^\circ \text{ C}\)

Oxidation and Nitridation Reaction

✦
\(AX_{(g)}\) \(+\) \({O_2}_{(g)}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(AO_{(s)}\) \(+\) \(2{H_2}_{(g)}\)
Example: \(SiO_2\) deposition from silane and oxygen at \(450^\circ \text{ C}\) lower than thermal oxidation.
✦
\({SiH_4}_{(g)}\) \(+\) \({O_2}_{(g)}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(Si_{(s)}\) \(+\) \(2{H_2}_{(g)}\)
Suitable to deposit: \(Al_2O_3\), \(TiO_2\), \(Ta_2O_5\), \(SnO_2\), \(ZnO\).

Exchange Reaction

Often using ammonia or water vapor.
✦
\(AX_{(g)}\) \(+\) \({NH_3}_{(g)}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(AN_{(S)}\) \(+\) \(HX_{(g)}\)
✦
\(AX_{g}\) \(+\) \(H_2O_{(g)}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(AO_{(S)}\) \(+\) \(HX_{(g)}\)
Example: deposition of resistant film (BN) at \(1100^\circ \text{ C}\).
✦
\({BF_3}_{(g)}\) \(+\) \({NH_3}_{(g)}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(BN_{(s)}\) \(+\) \(3HF_{(g)}\)
Suitable to deposit: \(TiN\), \(TaN\), \(AlN\), \(SiC\), \(Al_2O_3\), \(In_2O_3\), \(SnO_2\), \(SiO_2\)

Disproportionation Reaction

✦
\(2AB_{(g)}\) \(\rightleftharpoons\) \(A_{(s)}\) \(+\) \(AB_{g}\)
Compounds invovling elements with multiple valance states.
Suitable to deposit: \(Al\), \(C\), \(Ge\), \(SI\), III-V compounds.

Important Types of CVD

  • Low Pressure CVD (LPCVD)
  • Ultra high vaccum CVD (UHVCVD)
  • Plasma Enchanced CVD (PECVD)
  • Metal Organic CVD (MOCVD)

Plasma Enhanced CVD

(PECVD) is good for the deposition of dielectric thin films.
Example: \(SiN_X\), \(AlO_X\).
\(N_2 \text{ / }O_2\) gas plasma are used for reaction.
\(AuO_2\) from radical \(O_2\), \(GaN\) from radical \(N_2\).

Metal Orgonic CVD

Indium Phosphide (\(InP\)), III-V semiconductor, can be grown with Trimethylindium \((CH_3)_3 In\) and phosphine \(PH_3\) precursors.
