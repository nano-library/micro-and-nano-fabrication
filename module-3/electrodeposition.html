Electrochemical deposition

Electrochemical deposition is a process where the metallic ion can become solid metal and deposit on the cathode surface
  • Electrochemical deposition uses oxidation and reduction to accumulate one material onto the surface of another material
  • During the process, electricity is introduced to an electrolyte through the use of electrodes
  • After a while, the ions of the deposition material in the electrolyte adhere to one of the electrodes

Important components

  • Cathode
  • Power supply
  • Anode
  • Electrolyte
  • Ammeter
  • Voltmeter
  • Solution H\(_2\)O - \( M^+ \), \( A^- \)

Electrolyte

Metal salts (that can form ions in a solute, like in water)
  • AgCl
  • NiCl\(_2\)
  • CuSO\(_4\)

Cathode

Noble metals/inert metals (Au, Pt)

Anode

Usually made of the metal that is to be deposited or inert metals
